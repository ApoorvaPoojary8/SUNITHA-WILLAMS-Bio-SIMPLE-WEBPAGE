Main Biography Overview

Sunita Lyn Williams is a decorated American astronaut and United States Navy officer. She is known for holding the record for the longest spaceflight by a woman (195 days) at one time and for completing multiple spacewalks. Her journey from a naval aviator to a globally recognized astronaut is deeply inspiring, especially to young minds dreaming of space.

2. Naval Career and Training

Sunita became a helicopter pilot in the U.S. Navy and logged over 3,000 flight hours in more than 30 aircraft types. Her dedication and discipline led her to be selected as an astronaut candidate by NASA in 1998. She underwent two years of intensive training and was then assigned to technical duties before flying her first space mission.

3. Space Missions and Record

She flew her first space mission in 2006 aboard the space shuttle Discovery. During Expedition 14/15, she spent 195 days in space, setting a world record for women. She performed 7 spacewalks totaling over 50 hours. In 2012, she commanded the International Space Station during Expedition 33, becoming one of the few women to do so.
